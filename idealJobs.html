Ideal Jobs

Our group has a mix of ideal jobs and although there are 6 of us there are 4 unique ideas.

  • David and Adam have both believe System Admistration is the ideal job for them.
  • Craig is hoping to become a Cybersecurity Architect, and Jordan a Cybersecurity Analyst.
  • Hermanto would like to be an IT Director or CEO within the industry, somebody who directly impacts the course of a company.
  • Leigh would like to become a Software Developer, specifically using the .NET infrastructure.

Adam
Azure System Administrator		 - Between system administrators and cyber security architects there are some overlaps in general skillsets (such as comprehensive knowledge of the systems), but ultimately the two roles would work together. The cyber security architect can design a security plan under the direction of the administrator, and then the adminstrator can be the one to action the plan. The skillset shared between Azure System Administrators and System Administrators would be incredibly similar, however the Azure administrator is slightly more specialised. Both of these roles come with a great amount of responsibility, as the roles put them in charge of the systems used by the employer in their day to day operations. The Director of IT Operations & System Administrator roles overlap in the way that both of them are roles of great responsibility and impact. The director would be involved with some of the decision makings and lead the administrator on the right path, but the director would also be coordinating the efforts and stratergies of other departments to make sure everything worked in unison. The system administrator and security analyst are again similar in the fact they both need knowledge on the system they work with, but for different reasons. Whislt the administrator needs to know how the system works to make sure daily operations run smoothly, the security analyst needs to know the system so that it can prevent or respond to any security threats. The Azure system administrator and .NET Developer may have some overlap, as the both platforms are built by Microsoft and therefore integrate really well with each other. The administrator might not need to know as much about .NET, but as a developer having an understanding of the system you're targetting for production is critical.
