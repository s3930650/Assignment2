Having looked at the Burning Glass data, has your opinion of your ideal job changed? Why or why not?

Craig Scofield
After viewing Burning Glass's data, I believe I would still strive to obtain my ideal job. The skills required and the current and future demand for this position make it a desirable job. In addition, the area is still in its early years, and to be part of this field would be exciting.

Hermanto Surjani
Though it was a revelation to see the 'in demand' jobs rank, I do not believe I would change my ideal job. I believe if you are good at what you do, or will do, you will likely be secure and in demand. IT is a burgeoning field and I believe most roles will be in demand in the future. So why not choose a role that you are passionate about? I would love to do programming, but I would love more to impact the world within a leadership role. One can always indulge in other pursuits as a hobby, to enjoy and learn.

David Lardner
The Burning Glass data shows that my ideal job has no shortages currently but is expected to have strong future demand. I dont believe I would change my mind as my passion and interests are still the same. The data has highlighted a few more skills than I had originally thought the role would have required but it is still something I would want to do.

Leigh White
After reviewing the Burning Glass data, my desire to pursure my ideal job has only grown. As it's listed as being the highest in demand job, the security and availability for a career seems like it would almost be a guarantee with the right skillset. Even the skills I have in technologies like .NET and JavaScript are in high demand. Viewing this data has only confirmed for me that I am making the right career decisions by pursuing it.
