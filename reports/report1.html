Cloud, services, servers

Hermanto Surjani

An Introduction

What is a Cloud?
A cloud is a service, running on servers, that is accessed via a connection to the internet or a private network. Therefore, cloud services can be either accessed by anyone publicly, or in certain situations, it can be restricted in a more secure private cloud network. Depending on what the service requires. There are many multiple providers of cloud services, such as Amazon Web Services, Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud, Dropbox etc. There are companies that have embraced the cloud and have benefited from the online trade that it offers. Having an additional platform to sell their products allows them to reach to a wider audience. Examples are video streaming services like Disney+, news media such as Sydney Morning Herald, retail stores such as Myer and educational institutions such as the RMIT Online Courses. There are some companies have appeared with the opportunities that the cloud provides that have an cloud only presence. These include video streaming services like Netflix, music streaming provided by Spotify, transportation with Uber, AirBnB with accommodation and the list goes on. According to report by the International Data Group, 69% of businesses are using cloud technology and a further 18% plan to implement cloud solutions in the future. The cloud’s reach is profound and has changed industry and the way we live.

What is a service?
The services available from the cloud may be the serving of information, storage or computational /analysis services and often a combination of all three. It could be the website data that is provided from a cloud provider to you. This is also known as web traffic. It could also be other media such as audio, images or video. Computationally, the cloud provider may use their analysis to provide interactive websites, host web applications or provide the capability to analyse large sets data. The last of which is also coined “big data”. The cloud facilitates the increased use of insightful data analysis across almost every industry. One example of the cloud providing extreme processing capability, was for the calculation of Pi to 100,000,000,000,000 digits. This was performed by a Google research team that used the Google Cloud service to achieve the feat. Even with the cloud processing capability of Google, it still took almost 158 days to complete. And it required the use of 515 terabytes of storage.

What is a server?
“A cloud server is a pooled, centralized server resource that is hosted and delivered over a network—typically the Internet—and accessed on demand by multiple users. Cloud servers can perform all the same functions of a traditional physical server, delivering processing power, storage and applications.” Cloud servers can range in size, in terms of capability and physical dimensions. At an abstract level they are simply the hardware that powers the cloud. Large installations of servers are called a data centre. These data centres are located all over the world, in different geographic and political regions.

Reasons for the Cloud

Advantages of the cloud?
The advantages of cloud computing are the leveraging of datacentres, using the economies of scale to provide concentrated centres of computing, storage and connectivity capabilities.

It allows businesses to avoid capital expenditure CAPEX if they were to purchase servers, and instead use the cloud service as an operation expenditure OPEX.

This allows the flexibility of provisioning cloud services according to the needs of the businesses at any time. And not be limited by static server capability – if the business had to purchase the infrastructure itself.

It also allows the expertise of managing the technology to be situated and managed within the datacentres and not within each organisation.

The advantages of cloud computing are not limited to replacing large and powerful servers. One example of utilising the cloud is to empower small, lightweight devices with the capability of massive amounts of processing power.

Some examples include the GPS navigation application on your phone. Or the weather forecast analysis that is fed to your device. Or having the world’s knowledge at your fingertips.

Who provides the cloud?
There are several providers of cloud infrastructure. Two of the largest in 2022 are Amazon Web Services, AWS at 38.9% market share, and Microsoft Azure with 21.1%. Some cloud service providers provide online storage services such as iCloud, Google Drive and Dropbox. These providers have hundreds of millions of users.

What are the disadvantages of the cloud?
There are several disadvantages:

  • Security - Even with the investment in physical and network security, there are still vulnerabilities in any system. Terrorism may target data centres physically. While hackers may attack cloud providers, services and the people that rely on them.
  • Cost - For the customer, though the costs of using a cloud provider reduces CAPEX significantly. The nature of OPEX may mean some businesses end up paying more for server resources in the long term. For the cloud provider, there is a high cost of setting up datacentres.
  • connectivity - Depending on an online platform means that you need fast, reliable internet connectivity to continue using it effectively. While there are improvements being made continually to improve internet connectivity globally, some regions may not have a capable internet connection to rely on.

What is the impact of the cloud?
There is no doubt that the cloud has impacted industry greatly. It is estimated that in 2021, over 6.25 billion smartphones are in use globally. Companies that can leverage the reach that the cloud has can take advantage of additional methods of trade. Indeed the availability and capability of the cloud has created many businesses that are only available through online access. These have often disrupted long held ways of doing business in many industries. Music services such as Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube Music for example have encroached and replaced the way we listen to music. Video streaming services such as Netflix, Hulu and Youtube have supplanted television and cinemas as the primary avenue of watching television shows and movies. We can even play games using online gaming platforms. It has impacted almost everything we do. From the way we shop, the way we are entertained, even the way we find love. In a 2021 study in the United States, 46% of participants have used online dating platforms. (25 Online Dating Statistics & Trends in 2022)

What is the future of the cloud?
New technologies are constantly being researched and discovered. Developments in edge computing aims to bring the cloud technologies closer to the source. Facilitating speed and reliability. Improvements in wearable technology, such as smartwatches and the expected introduction of smart glasses will continue to leverage the cloud. To bring technology ever closer to being not just an extension of the human body, but a part of it. Further advances in the power and accessibility of the cloud may enable the holy grail of all the knowledge and data in the world, to be available to any device, anywhere in the world instantaneously
