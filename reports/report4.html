Autonomous Vehicles

Adam Greenwood

What is an autonomous vehicle?
An autonomous vehicle is one that can drive itself from point A to point B in autopilot mode using various in vehicle technologies and sensors, including adaptive cruise control, active steering, anti-lock braking systems, GPS navigation technology, lasers, radars and artificial intelligence.

What is the state of the art of this new technology and what can be down now?

  • Level 1 - Driver Assistance: At this level, the automated systems start to take control of the vehicle in specific situations, but do not fully take over. An example is adaptive cruised control which controls acceleration and breaking, typically in highway driving. Depending on the functionality drivers are able to take their feet off the pedals.
  • Level 2 - Partial Automation: Advanced driver assistance systems (ADAS) are a key feature of level 2. These cars can brake automatically, accelerate and take over steering. However, theses ae not fully autonomous cars a driver continues to remain in control and must always pay attention to traffic.
  • Level 3 - Conditional Automation: At level 3, divers can disengage from the act of driving but only in specific situations. Conditions could be limited to certain vehicle speeds, road types and weather conditions. But because drivers can apply their focus to some other tasks such as looking at a phone or newspaper this is generally considered the initial entry point to autonomous driving. Never less the driver is expected to take over when the system requests it. For example, features such as traffic jam pilot mean that drivers can sit back and relax while the system handles it all, acceleration, steering and breaking in stop and go traffic, the vehicle sends an alert to the driver to regain control when the vehicle gets through the traffic jam and vehicle speed increases.
  • Level 4 - High Automation: At this level the vehicle’s autonomous driving system is fully capable of monitoring the driving environment and handling all driving functions for routine routes and conditions defined within its operational design domain (ODD). The vehicle mat alert the driver that is reaching its operational limits if there is say an environmental condition that requires a human in control such as heavy snow. If the driver doesn’t respond it will secure the vehicle automatically.
  • Level 5 - Full Automation: Level 5 capable vehicles are fully autonomous. No diver is required behind the wheel at all. In fact, level 5 vehicles might not even have a steering wheel or gas/brake pedals. These vehicles could have smart cabins so that passengers can issue voice commands to choose a destination or set cabin conditions such as temperature or choice of media.

Let us break this process down into three main components:
We are building autonomous vehicles to drive themselves but we what them to drive like human drivers would drive. This means we need o proved these vehicles with sensory functions, cognitive functions, memory, logical thinking decision-making, execute capabilities the humans use to drive vehicles. In-vehicle data collection and communication systems

  • Component 1 - In-Vehicle data collection & communication systems:
    Autonomous vehicles are fitted with numerous sensors, radars and cameras to generate massive amounts of environmental data all of these form the digital sensorium through which the autonomous vehicle can see, hear and see the road, other vehicles and every other object on/near the road just like a human driver would pay attention to the road while driving.
  • Component 2 - Autonomous driving platform (cloud):
    The autonomous driving platform which is in the cloud contains an intelligent agent which makes use of AI algorithms to make meaningful decisions. It acts as the control policy or the brain of the autonomous vehicle. This intelligent agent is also connected to a database to which acts as a memory where past driving experiences are stored.
  • Component 3 - AI-based function in autonomous vehicles:
    Based on the decisions made by the intelligent agent. The autonomous vehicle can detect objects on the road, manoeuvre through traffic without human intervention and gets to the destination safely.

This data loop called perception action cycle takes place repetitively, the more the number of perception action cycles take place, that more intelligent the intelligent agent becomes resulting in higher accuracy of making decisions especially in complex driving situations. Artificial intelligence especially neural networks and deep learning have become an absolute necessity to make autonomous vehicles function properly and safely.
